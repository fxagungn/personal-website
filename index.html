Claude vs ChatGPT vs Gemini: How Each AI Fits Into My L&D Ecosystem

FX Agung Nugroho · 18 Februari 2026 · 9 menit baca

I want to start by saying something that most AI comparisons skip: these tools are not competitors in my daily work. They're collaborators with different personalities. Over more than a year of using all three every day in an L&D context, I've developed a clear sense of when to reach for each one. and that clarity has made me significantly more effective.

This isn't a technical benchmark. It's a practitioner's field report.

Claude: My Primary Thinking Partner

Claude is where I go when the work requires depth. Designing a multi-week leadership development program, writing a 20-slide instructional framework with full speaker notes, building a KPI monitoring system, drafting strategic proposals for key decision-makers. these all live in Claude.

What sets it apart for L&D work is the quality of its reasoning under constraint. When I give Claude complex context. "design a training module for operational managers who are resistant to change, in a multi-brand F&B environment, using Kirkpatrick Level 2-3 evaluation". it doesn't just execute the request. It thinks through it. The output feels like it came from someone who understood the problem, not just the prompt.

Claude also has a quality I deeply value: it pushes back when something doesn't make sense. It has raised blind spots in my program designs that I hadn't considered. and it consistently produces strategic documents and proposals that resonate with key stakeholders. That intellectual honesty is rare and genuinely useful in a thinking partner.

"Claude doesn't just answer my questions. It helps me figure out if I'm asking the right ones."

ChatGPT: My Creative Sprinting Tool

When I need to generate a lot of options fast. ten icebreaker ideas for a leadership program, five different framings for a difficult message, rapid variations on a training activity. ChatGPT is my first call. Its strength is creative velocity. It moves quickly and broadly, which is exactly what you need during the divergent thinking phase of content design.

It's also the tool my team is most comfortable with, which matters when I'm trying to model AI adoption. Familiarity lowers the friction of getting started, and ChatGPT has done more than any other tool to normalize AI use among non-technical professionals.

Where I find it less reliable: sustained, nuanced work that requires holding a lot of context. It can drift. For a two-page email, perfect. For a 20-page instructional framework, I'll use Claude.

Gemini: My Research & Google-Connected Workhorse

Gemini earns its place primarily because of two things: its integration with the Google ecosystem, and its strength in research synthesis. When I need to pull together information from multiple sources and surface a clear picture quickly, Gemini is fast and reliable. It's also significantly more capable when working with content that lives in Google Drive, Gmail, or Google Docs. which is where a large portion of our L&D documentation lives.

For daily operational research. "what are current best practices for onboarding programs in distributed workforces?". Gemini gives me a solid first pass that I can then take deeper in Claude. I think of it as my intelligence gathering layer.

The Real Lesson: It's an Ecosystem, Not a Competition

The practitioners I see struggling with AI are usually those who picked one tool and are trying to make it do everything. The ones who are thriving have built a workflow where different tools play different roles. and they've taken the time to understand what each one is actually good at.

In my workflow: I use Gemini to gather and orient, ChatGPT to diverge and generate, and Claude to synthesize, deepen, and produce. Not because one is "better". but because each one makes a different part of my work better.

That's what AI literacy actually looks like in practice. Not picking a winner. Building a team.

Yang membedakannya untuk pekerjaan L&D adalah kualitas penalarannya di bawah kendala. Ketika saya memberi Claude konteks yang kompleks, ia tidak hanya mengeksekusi permintaan. Ia memikirkannya. Praktisi yang berjuang dengan AI biasanya mereka yang memilih satu alat dan mencoba membuatnya melakukan segalanya. Yang berhasil telah membangun alur kerja di mana alat berbeda memainkan peran berbeda.

Dalam alur kerja saya: Gemini untuk mengumpulkan dan mengorientasi, ChatGPT untuk divergen dan menghasilkan, Claude untuk mensintesis, memperdalam, dan memproduksi. Bukan karena satu "lebih baik". tetapi karena masing-masing membuat bagian berbeda dari pekerjaan saya lebih baik. Itulah literasi AI yang sesungguhnya. Bukan memilih pemenang. Membangun tim.

LMS · Moodle · Digital Learning

From Admin Tool to Learning Engine: How We Reimagined Moodle

FX Agung Nugroho · 7 March 2026 · 8 min read

When I first inherited our Moodle setup, it was doing exactly what most LMS platforms do in their early stages: storing files and logging completions. PDFs uploaded. Quizzes ticked. Certificates generated. Nobody was actually learning more because of it. they were just getting through it.

That's the quiet failure mode of most digital learning implementations: the system works perfectly, but it doesn't change behavior. And in an organization spanning hundreds of operational locations, a system that doesn't change behavior at scale is just expensive overhead.

The Diagnosis: Three Root Problems

  • Content disconnected from context. Modules designed in isolation from real work environments.
  • Completion was the only metric. No visibility into skill gaps. No feedback loop for improvement.
  • The platform felt like homework. Learners accessed Moodle when they had to. not because they found value.

What We Changed

"The platform is not the learning. It's the infrastructure. We had to stop optimizing the infrastructure and start optimizing the experience."

We reorganized the course library around job roles and performance outcomes. Built a custom reporting layer connecting learning activity to on-the-job performance indicators. Redesigned the learner interface with a mobile-first approach and introduced scenario-based assessments that mirrored real situations.

The Harder Lesson

The most significant obstacle wasn't technical. It was behavioral. Getting trainers to think in outcomes, managers to see themselves as learning enablers, and learners to view the platform as a growth tool. that required sustained change management no platform update could automate.

The platform is infrastructure. The culture is the product. The question isn't "are we using it?". it's "is it making people measurably better at their jobs?"

Dari Admin Tool ke Learning Engine: Bagaimana Kami Merombak Moodle

FX Agung Nugroho · 7 Maret 2026 · 8 menit baca

Ketika saya pertama kali mewarisi pengaturan Moodle kami, platform itu melakukan apa yang kebanyakan LMS lakukan di tahap awal: menyimpan file dan mencatat penyelesaian. PDF diunggah. Kuis dicentang. Sertifikat dihasilkan. Tidak ada yang benar-benar belajar lebih karena itu. mereka hanya menyelesaikannya.

Itulah mode kegagalan tersembunyi dari sebagian besar implementasi pembelajaran digital: sistem bekerja dengan sempurna, tetapi tidak mengubah perilaku.

Diagnosis: Tiga Masalah Akar

  • Konten terputus dari konteks. Modul dirancang terpisah dari lingkungan kerja nyata.
  • Penyelesaian adalah satu-satunya metrik. Tidak ada visibilitas ke kesenjangan keterampilan.
  • Platform terasa seperti PR. Pelajar mengakses Moodle ketika mereka harus. bukan karena mereka menemukan nilai.

Yang Kami Ubah

"Platform bukan pembelajaran. Ia adalah infrastruktur. Kami harus berhenti mengoptimalkan infrastruktur dan mulai mengoptimalkan pengalaman."

Kami mereorganisasi perpustakaan kursus di sekitar peran pekerjaan dan hasil kinerja. Membangun lapisan pelaporan kustom yang menghubungkan aktivitas pembelajaran dengan indikator kinerja di tempat kerja. Merancang ulang antarmuka pelajar dengan pendekatan mobile-first.

Pelajaran yang Lebih Sulit

Hambatan terbesar bukan teknis. Melainkan perilaku. Membuat trainer berpikir dalam hasil, manajer melihat diri sebagai learning enabler, dan pelajar melihat platform sebagai alat pertumbuhan. itu membutuhkan manajemen perubahan yang tidak bisa diotomatiskan oleh pembaruan platform apapun.

Platform adalah infrastruktur. Budaya adalah produknya.

AI · L&D Strategy

Working with AI Without Losing the Human Element

FX Agung Nugroho · 22 January 2026 · 6 min read

The honest version of this starts with an admission: when I first began using AI tools regularly, I was looking for shortcuts. What I found was something more interesting. a forcing function for clearer thinking.

Where AI Genuinely Helps

First-draft generation. Curriculum outlines, training scripts, assessment frameworks. AI reduces the time-to-first-draft by an order of magnitude. It removes the blank page problem.

Perspective expansion. I now use AI to challenge my assumptions before I finalize any design. "What would a skeptical learner say about this module?". these prompts surface blind spots that would otherwise require hours of stakeholder interviews.

Documentation at scale. L&D teams are chronically under-resourced for documentation. AI enables higher-quality outputs without expanding headcount.

Where AI Falls Short

AI cannot replace the relational work of L&D. It cannot read the emotional undercurrent in a coaching session. It cannot build the credibility that comes from a trainer who has done the job they're training others for. It cannot design the trust that makes people willing to be vulnerable about their development gaps.

Building an AI-Literate Team

The real opportunity: not just adopting AI yourself, but building organizational AI literacy as a core capability. Make AI adoption safe to experiment with. Managing a Nationwide Learning Network: What Scale Taught Me About Trust

FX Agung Nugroho · 3 February 2026 · 5 min read

At some point in every organization's L&D journey, the question shifts from "are we running good training?" to "how do we run good training everywhere, all the time, without being in the room?"

The Illusion of Centralized Control

My first instinct was to build a tight, centralized system. Standardized scripts. Mandatory sequences. Central sign-off on content. It felt rigorous. It was actually fragile. Centralized control optimizes for consistency at the expense of relevance.

The Network Model

The shift that worked: from a delivery model to a network model. We invested in Learning Partners as learning leaders in their own right. Frameworks, not scripts. Principles, not mandates. Feedback loops that let local insight flow back to the center.

"The job of a central L&D function at scale is not to control the experience. It's to raise the capability of the people closest to the learner."

This requires genuine trust in people you may never meet in person. It requires designing for human variability rather than eliminating it. And it requires the humility to accept that the best version of your program will be one you didn't fully design. it emerged from the network.

What This Means Beyond Any Industry

Any organization building capability at scale faces the same tension: how do you maintain coherence without suffocating the local intelligence that makes your frontline effective? Invest relentlessly in the human infrastructure. and then get out of their way.
